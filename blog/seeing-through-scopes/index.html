Seeing Through Scopes

Concept

Designed for the science museum's medical gallery. This interactive was built to simulate a gastroscopy procedure.

the user finds a point of interest - the uvula

Gameplay

PLayers feed a tube representing a gastroscope in and out of the mouth of a fibreglass model of a patient lying on an operating table. They also have a joystick to control to pan and tilt the camera, and two buttons. One button zooms the camera, the other squirts some water to clean the lens.

the player is heading the wrong way and must turn back

The objective is to make it down to the stomach and detect all points of interest on the way. The users current position is displayed on the map, along with any discovered points of interest. Nearby points which have been missed are displayed as an outline.

contextual help lets the player know they may have missed something

A pop-up in the bottom left gives a description when a player enters a new area and when they discover a point of interest.

Contextual hints are shown at the top of the screen if the player is struggling or has missed something.

the player enters a new area - the stomach

Technology

Hardware

  • Custom built gastroscope control uses a ring of tubing to allow the player to feed it into and out of the mouth
  • Heavy duty joystick and buttons
  • Windows PC
  • HD Display

Software

  • Main game developed in Unity
  • Custom interface for the gastroscope control
  • Custom interface for the joystick and buttons

the player finds another point of interest - a stomach ulcer

Development

All software was developed by me, the main game in Unity/C# and the control interfaces in C++. The 3D model was created by an artist on my team with some additional shader and particle effects produced by me.
