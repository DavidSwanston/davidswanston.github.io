Shape Your City

Concept

Designed and built for the Future Engineers gallery at London Transport Museum, this project aims to show players how difficult it is to design and balance a city. The only way to succeed is to work together.

planning phase begins

Gameplay

The game is ideally played with 4 players, one per section of the stylized version of London. If fewer than 4 players start the game, the remaining players are controlled by the computer which will pick random values.

planning phase in play

In the first phase of the game, players are shown a blueprint version of the city and can spend their limited resources by turning dials on their control panel. Players can spend resources in one of three areas:

  • Connected: Adds tube and bus stops
  • Well Being: Adds markets and attractions
  • Greener: Adds charging points and pedestrianised zones

simulation phase begins

In the second phase, a simulation of the city is run and players find how their decisions effect the city. This is where they may realise that their choices have consequences outside their own area.

A player may have overspent on wellbeing, but underspent on connected, leading to increased traffic in other areas. Alternatively, a player may have added too many pedestrianised zones meaning traffic has to make detours into other areas of the city.

At the end of the simulation, a breakdown of "happiness" is shown for each zone and an overall happiness percentage is show on the second screen.

Technology

Hardware

  • Custom built table with projection surface
  • Custom built control panels with dials and buttons
  • Ceiling mounted projector
  • Windows PC
  • Second display to show views of the city

Software

  • Main game developed in Unity
  • Custom C++ interface for control panels

Development

I designed and developed this project solo from the initial concept to install, and worked closely with the team at London Transport Museum through the whole process.

The 3d assets were created from scratch by an artist on my team. I got him to create the individual buildings and landmarks on a grid system, then build tools to place the assets and build the city.
