Tunnel Blast (NAX)

Concept

Designed and built for the training area of Nerf Action Xperience - Manchester, the idea behind this game was to teach players to work toward a common goal.

gameplay

Gameplay

A group of players have 60 seconds to navigate a network of tunnels. Using Nerf blasters they must hit moving targets to increase their score and replenish the ever depleting energy bar. A bonus multiplier is applied for getting a streak of hits.

Energy directly relates to speed through the tunnels, the more targets the players hit the faster they go.

Players will encounter branching paths in the tunnel network. Here time slows and the have a few seconds to decide to select the path forward. Not all paths are equal, so some trial and error is required for the best scores.

After players complete the tunnel, or run out of time, a map of their progress and score is displayed.

Technology

Hardware

  • Runs on Windows PC hardware
  • Short throw projector display
  • Darts detected by IR touch frame, providing touch inputs

Software

  • Developed in Unity
  • Hardware (start) button input provided as UDP packet
  • UDP packets sent to a show controller to signal events

game over screen

Development

Tunnel Blast was a solo developed project, with UI artwork provided by the NAX design team. Although the gameplay is simple, it was a design challenge to make the game playable with limited and often inaccurate inputs (a dart fired at a surface).
