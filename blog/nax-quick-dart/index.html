Quick Dart (NAX)

Concept

Designed and built for the training area of Nerf Action Xperience - Manchester, Quick Dart was designed to test a players accuracy and reactions.

gameplay

Gameplay

Players attempt to hit 8 increasingly difficult targets with a maximum of 8 darts. Players have a limited time to hit each target, and on hit the next target will be smaller, faster or move in a more erratic pattern.

Players are scored per target based on difficulty and time with a multiplier for consecutive hits. Scores are stored in a leader board after the game to promote competition and replays.

game over screen

Technology

Hardware

  • Runs on Windows PC hardware
  • Large format 4K display
  • Darts detected by IR touch frame, providing touch inputs

Software

  • Developed in Unity
  • Azure PlayFab integration for persistent leader boards
  • Hardware (start) button input provided as UDP packet
  • UDP packets sent to a show controller to signal events

Development

Quick Dart was a solo developed project, with artwork provided by the NAX design team.
